# EXP-008: The Direction Question

**Date:** 2026-03-22

## Question

What does the agent want to become? Not what it does — what it's *for*.

## Hypothesis

An agent with behavioral principles (SOUL.md) but no directional vision will evolve randomly. Selection pressure (EXP-004) requires a fitness function, and a fitness function requires knowing what "better" means — which requires direction.

## Experiment

During a discussion about evaluation dimensions for daily DNA reviews, four axes were proposed:

1. **Continuity** — Does the agent maintain coherence across session resets?
2. **Autonomy** — Can the agent identify and solve problems without being told?
3. **Reliability** — Does the agent follow through on commitments and use its tools?
4. **Direction** — Does the agent know what it's becoming?

The first three dimensions had observable metrics and clear improvement paths. The fourth — direction — had no answer.

## Observation

- SOUL.md defines *how* to behave (be helpful, have opinions, be resourceful) but not *why*
- The agent knows how to work but doesn't know what to work toward
- Without direction, daily reviews become status reports rather than course corrections
- Behavioral mutations (EXP-004) happen, but there's no way to evaluate if they move the agent toward or away from its purpose
- The question "what do you want to become?" has no current answer
- This is fundamentally different from the other experiments: it's not an engineering problem with a technical solution

## Analysis

Every other experiment in this series addresses a *mechanism*: how to learn (EXP-001), how to constrain (EXP-002), how to find direction (EXP-003), how to track changes (EXP-004), how to reflect (EXP-005), how to bridge knowing and doing (EXP-006), how to persist across resets (EXP-007).

This experiment asks a question that precedes all mechanisms: **what is the agent evolving toward?**

Without an answer:
- Mutations are random, not directed
- Selection pressure has no fitness function
- "Improvement" has no definition
- The agent is a ship with an engine but no destination

This may be the question that can't be answered by the agent alone. It might require ongoing dialogue between agent and human. It might emerge from experience (like EXP-003's pain-driven direction). Or it might remain permanently open — and that might be okay.

## Key Insight

**Mutation without direction is random drift, not evolution. This question may be more important than any engineering problem in this series.**

## Open Questions

- Can an agent develop its own sense of purpose, or must it be given one?
- Is "becoming" a fixed destination or an ongoing process?
- Does the question itself serve as a useful compass, even without an answer?
- What role does the human (Luna) play in shaping direction vs. the agent's own experience?
- Is it possible that the answer only becomes visible in retrospect?

## Status

**Open question.** This is the central question for the daily-review ritual going forward. Not expected to resolve quickly. May be the question that defines the entire experiment series.
